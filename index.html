The Science, Simply Explained

What Is GLP-1?

GLP-1 stands for Glucagon-Like Peptide-1 — a hormone your gut already produces after eating. GLP-1 medications amplify this natural signal.

Appetite Suppression

GLP-1 activates receptors in the hypothalamus — your brain's satiety center — reducing hunger signals. Most patients report thinking about food significantly less, even between meals.

Slowed Gastric Emptying

GLP-1 slows how fast your stomach empties after eating. You feel full longer. Blood sugar spikes flatten out. Cravings for high-carb foods may decrease noticeably.

Improved Insulin Response

GLP-1 stimulates your pancreas to release insulin — but only when blood sugar is actually elevated. This glucose-dependent action makes it precise and may lower hypoglycemia risk.

Metabolic Reset Potential

Over months of therapy, many patients see improvements in blood pressure, cholesterol, and A1C — benefits that may persist even after tapering, especially when combined with dietitian-guided lifestyle changes.

The key insight: In people with obesity or metabolic dysfunction, the natural GLP-1 response is often blunted. Medication fills that gap — giving your biology the signal it may not be producing effectively on its own.
